Baklava

Description

A delectable Middle Eastern dessert, baklava is made by layering sheets of buttered phyllo dough, generously sprinkled with a mixture of finely chopped nuts, usually a blend of walnuts, pistachios, and/or almonds. Between the layers, a sweet and aromatic combination of sugar, cinnamon, and cloves is added, creating a rich and flavorful filling.

Once assembled, the pastry is baked until golden brown and crisp. After baking, a fragrant honey and lemon syrup is poured over the hot baklava, allowing it to absorb the sweet essence. The result is a heavenly combination of crunchy layers, nutty texture, and sweet, syrupy perfection.

Ingridients:

  • Phyllo dough
  • Unsalted butter
  • Mixed nuts
  • Granulated sugar
  • Honey
  • Lemon juice

Steps:

  1. Prepare Nut Filling: Mix finely chopped mixed nuts with sugar, cinnamon, and ground cloves.
  2. Layer Phyllo Dough: Brush melted butter between layers of phyllo dough in a baking dish.
  3. Add Nut Filling: Sprinkle nut mixture over the phyllo layers.
  4. Repeat Layers: Continue layering phyllo and nuts, brushing with melted butter.
  5. Cut and Bake: Cut the layered baklava into squares or diamonds and bake until golden brown.
  6. Prepare Syrup: Boil honey, water, sugar, and lemon juice to create a syrup.
  7. Pour Syrup: Pour hot syrup over the hot baklava as soon as it's out of the oven.
  8. Cool and Serve: Allow baklava to cool completely before serving.
